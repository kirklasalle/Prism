# PRISM Licensing and Brand Appendix

Date: 2026-03-17  
Audience: Product, Legal, Commercial, Partnerships

## 1. Brand Architecture

PRISM brand structure is two-profile, one-platform:

- **PRISM Individual**: capability-first operator experience.
- **PRISM Business**: governance-first enterprise operating profile.

Brand invariant:

- both profiles share the same core capability surface;
- Business profile adds mandatory governance, auditability, and approval controls.

## 2. Licensing Strategy Framework

## 2.1 Licensing objectives

- preserve broad adoption in individual/developer segments,
- capture enterprise value through governance and compliance depth,
- avoid product-line fragmentation where Business appears functionally weaker.

## 2.2 Recommended licensing posture

### Option A (recommended): Capability Parity, Governance Depth as Commercial Differentiator

- Individual license:
  - full capability surface with individual-suitable defaults,
  - optional advanced governance modules.
- Business license:
  - full capability surface,
  - mandatory governance modules,
  - policy bundles, approval APIs, audit export, compliance operations support.

Why recommended:

- aligns to product invariant (no capability subtraction in Business),
- protects enterprise value via control/compliance assurances,
- simplifies messaging and adoption path.

### Option B: Capability Gating by Profile

- selected high-end capabilities restricted to Business.

Tradeoff:

- can increase upsell pressure,
- risks weakening developer/individual adoption and ecosystem momentum.

## 3. Packaging Model

Proposed commercial packaging:

1. Core Runtime Package
   - orchestrator, policy engine, memory framework, baseline adapters.
2. Parity Capability Package
   - terminal virtualization, container orchestration, dynamic tool staging, adapter pack compatibility framework.
3. Agent Orchestration Package
   - agent lifecycle management, per-agent model assignment, swarm coordination (4 topologies), intelligent telemetry, chat-to-agent routing.
4. Governance Assurance Package (Business mandatory)
   - tier enforcement controls, approval workflows, governance telemetry, replay/export support.
5. Operations & Compliance Package
   - incident/runbook tooling, policy diff/export, evidence bundle automation.

## 4. Policy and Compliance Positioning

Commercial promise for Business profile:

- no high-risk operation without policy determination,
- no approval-gated action without explicit decision trace,
- no production release without evidence artifacts mapped to claims.

Compliance-facing differentiators:

- operational traceability,
- deterministic governance pathways,
- replay-ready incident analysis.

## 5. Ecosystem and Adapter Licensing

Adapter ecosystem design goals:

- encourage third-party innovation,
- maintain compatibility and trust,
- preserve business-grade safety posture.

Proposed adapter policy:

- manifest-based capability declarations,
- semantic version and compatibility checks,
- trust metadata and signed distribution channels for Business installs.

## 6. Go-to-Market Messaging Guardrails

Required message consistency:

- PRISM Individual: "top-tier capability, fast operator experience with intelligent multi-agent orchestration."
- PRISM Business: "same capability with verifiable governance, audit control, and governed swarm intelligence."

Avoid statements implying:

- Business is feature-reduced,
- governance is optional in enterprise profile,
- parity claims without implementation evidence.

## 7. Decision Checklist

Before licensing finalization:

1. Confirm profile capability equivalence matrix.
2. Confirm governance modules mapped to business SKU requirements.
3. Confirm pricing narrative aligns with control/compliance value, not artificial feature subtraction.
4. Confirm legal review of adapter pack trust model and distribution terms.
5. Confirm investor-facing claims align to implemented milestones and release artifacts.
